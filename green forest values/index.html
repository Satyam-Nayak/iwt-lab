    Top 5

    Meditational Plants

    Aloe Vera

    Aloe Vera is often referred to as the King of plants in the Ayurvedic texts. The usefulness of Aloe Vera can be determined by the fact that it can be incorporated into our skin care, hair care, and health care routine. You can include it in drinks to improve your gut health and immunity.

    Coriander

    Like mint, coriander is also used extensively for cooking in India. Apart from giving a refreshing aroma and taste to your recipes, it also works as a food preservative. Including it in your daily diet keeps your digestive system running. It is also considered to be useful in regulating the menstrual cycle.

    Fenugreek

    Fenugreek or methi as it is popularly called in India is one of the most useful herbal plants. It is also eaten as a vegetable. It curbs hair loss, controls high blood pressure, boosts appetite, and strengthens joints. Regular consumption of Fenugreek as a vegetable can heal joint pain. It is also suggested for those who suffer from diabetes.

    Mint

    Mint is a popular medicinal plant that is used as a medicine and in cuisines. Its fresh and invigorating fragrance proves to be an instant mood refresher. Its medicinal uses include its ability to restore respiratory health. It also repels insects and pests and helps you in keeping your surroundings clean and happy.

    Tulsi

    Tulsi is regarded as an auspicious plant in the Hindu religion. It also holds great significance in Ayurveda due to its amazing medical uses. It balances the tri doshas i.e. Vata, Pita, and Kapha elements of the human body.
