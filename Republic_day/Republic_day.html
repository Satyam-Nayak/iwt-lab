Republic-Day

What is INDIA's Republic Day ?

India's Republic Day is a national holiday celebrated on January 26th each year to commemorate the adoption of the Constitution of India. This day marks the transition of India from a British Dominion to a Republic. On this day, the President of India unfurls the national flag in the presence of a large number of people. Republic Day is celebrated with grand military parades and marches in national capital New Delhi. The parade showcases India's military strength, social and cultural diversity and cultural heritage. People of different states dress in their traditional attires and perform folk dances. The parade is concluded with a spectacular display of fireworks. On this day, people exchange sweets and gifts and visit each other's places. People of India remember this day as a reminder of the sacrifices made by the freedom fighters who fought for the independence of India. This day is also a symbol of unity and diversity among the citizens of India.

How is INDIA's Republic Day celebrated?

India's Republic Day is celebrated every year on January 26th, in commemoration of the day when the Constitution of India was adopted in 1950. This day is celebrated with great pomp and show across the country, with parades and other events. Flag hoisting ceremonies take place in all the state and union territories, where the Prime Minister of India pays homage to the soldiers who have sacrificed their lives to protect the country. Furthermore, a grand parade is also held in the capital city of New Delhi, showcasing India's military might, cultural heritage, and progress in different fields. There are also cultural programmes and performances that are held in different cities and towns, where people come together to celebrate the country's unity. The day also serves as an opportunity for people from all walks of life to come together and pledge to uphold the values of the Indian Constitution and strive for the betterment of the nation.
